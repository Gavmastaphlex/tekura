    Payment and registration

    (January – May 2017 instructions)

    2017 NCEA payment and registration

    You will be required to pay an annual NCEA fee so that your 2017 credits can be included in your Record of Achievement and count towards your qualifications.

    Check back here mid-June for instructions.

    Checking your 2016 NCEA results

    Your 2016 NCEA results became available in your NZQA student login page from 13 January 2017. Check your results online as NZQA does not distribute paper result notices.

    If all your 2016 results are not showing in your Record of Achievement, check you paid your 2016 NCEA fee. If some of your 2016 results are missing from your Record of Achievement, urgently contact your subject teacher on 0800 659 988.

    If you did not pay your 2016 NCEA fee

    If you gained credits in 2016 but did not pay your 2016 NCEA fee, you need to pay NZQA to have your results recorded in your Record of Achievement. This will include an additional late payment fee.

    Go to your NZQA student login page and follow the instructions in the Fees Payment link. You can pay by credit card online, by bank deposit or you can ask for an NZQA Candidate Fee form to be posted to you by contacting the Qualifications Team at Te Kura on 0800 659 988 (select option 3).

    Ordering a certificate and/or Record of Achievement

    To receive an official copy of an awarded certificate and/or your Record of Achievement, place an order through your NZQA student login page. It is recommended you do not order a Record of Achievement until after April if you have applied for a review or reconsideration. The first copy of each document is free and subsequent copies cost $15.30.

    Reviews and reconsiderations of external standards

    Following the return of your marked NCEA exam booklets by NZQA from late January, you have until 19 February to apply for any reviews or reconsiderations. Full details are included on the coloured information sheet returned with your marked exam booklets. The final date for a review/reconsideration of an NZ Scholarship standard is 4 March.

    Need more help?

    Many of your questions will be covered in our FAQ. However, if you still need help, contact your learning advisor or the Qualifications Team on 0800 65 99 88 (select option 3).

    (June – October 2017 instructions)

    Payment and registration

    You are required to pay an annual NCEA fee so that your 2017 credits can be included in your Record of Achievement and count towards your qualifications.

    How do I pay?

    Your NCEA fee can be paid online through our NCEA Payment and Registration. Te Kura collects this annual fee on behalf of the New Zealand Qualifications Authority (NZQA).

    To pay online you’ll need your:

    • Te Kura student ID number
    • credit card

    If you do not pay by credit card, our NCEA bank account details are emailed to you on submission of your online registration.

    What to remember

    • You must pay to register your NCEA credits in the year you gain them
    • Your NCEA payment is not an end of year exam fee
    • Your NCEA payment is on top of any enrolment administration fee you may have paid to Te Kura
    • If you are enrolled as a young adult, your Te Kura enrolment is free but you must still pay to register your NCEA credits.

    Key information

    Refer to our FAQ page to find out about:

    • how much to pay
    • financial assistance to reduce the fee
    • the importance of registering your credits
    • how to register to sit end of year exams
    • photo ID for end of year exams
    • junior students gaining NCEA credits
    • students also enrolled with another secondary school

    You may be eligible for special assessment conditions if you have a specific physical, medical, sensory or learning disability. Find out how to apply.

    What if I want to enrol at university or other tertiary provider for semester two?

    If you are enrolling at university or other tertiary provider for semester two this year, you must phone Jo Parmenter on 0800 65 99 88 extn 8850 before you complete an online registration or pay your 2017 NCEA fee (this does not apply if you are enrolling for university at the beginning of 2018). There are special instructions you will need to follow for the early release of your results for UE.

    What if I study at a face-to-face school too?

    If you study with us but are also a student at a face-to-face secondary school, your NCEA registration and payment will be managed by your own school. This means you don’t need to complete the online registration with Te Kura.

    (November 2017 – January 2018 instructions)

    Payment and registration

    Te Kura no longer processing 2017 NCEA payment and registration

    Te Kura’s deadline for processing NCEA payment and registration this year has closed. If you have not yet paid your 2017 fee, you must now pay NZQA.

    2017 NCEA fees must now be paid to NZQA

    If you require your 2017 results to be available in your Record of Achievement and count towards your qualifications, you need to make your payment to NZQA.

    Go to your NZQA student login page and follow the instructions (payment can be made by credit card or bank deposit) or you can request an NZQA Candidate Fee form be posted to you by contacting the Qualifications Team at Te Kura on 0800 659 988 (select option 3).

    The fee is $76.70 for most people. However if the person paying the fee is receiving a Work and Income or StudyLink benefit, or has a Community Services card please contact the Qualifications Team at Te Kura immediately on 0800 65 99 88 (select option 3).

    Please do not send your payment to Te Kura as we can no longer process it against your 2017 results.

    Will I have to pay a late fee to NZQA?

    Your payment must reach NZQA before 1 December 2017 to avoid the additional $50 late payment fee.

    What if I only recently enrolled with Te Kura?

    You will need to pay the 2017 NCEA fee to NZQA if you intend gaining NCEA results this year as you must pay for your NCEA results in the year they are awarded. Your payment must still reach NZQA prior to 1 December to avoid the additional $50 late payment fee.

    I’m a student at a secondary school also studying Te Kura courses – do I have to pay the NCEA fee?

    Your NCEA payment should have been managed by your own school.

    Need more help?

    Many of your questions will be covered in our FAQ. However, if you still need help, contact your learning advisor or the Qualifications Team on 0800 65 99 88.
