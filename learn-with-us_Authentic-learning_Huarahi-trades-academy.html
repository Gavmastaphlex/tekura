    Huarahi Trades Academy

    Te Kura offers trades programmes around New Zealand for students in year 11 and above. They are:

    • practical and hands-on programmes with tertiary organisations such as polytechnics and private training organisations
    • usually face to face, one or two days a week.

    The programmes can lead to NCEA Level 2, further training and study towards your goals, or to a career. To participate you must be a full-time student with Te Kura, studying three subjects at a minimum of NCEA Level 1 and be committed to completing the programme over one or two years.

    How does it work?

    You will do courses for one or two days a week with one of our partners. Some programmes will involve a work placement.

    Our partners include:

    • Ara Institute of Canterbury – Ashburton, Christchurch, Oamaru, Timaru
    • Bay of Plenty Trades Academy (BOP Polytech) – Bay of Plenty
    • Enviroskills – Northland and Rotorua
    • G and H Trade Training – North Island wide (see our flyers for information on carpentry and automotive training) – East Tamaki, Hamilton, Napier, North Shore, Palmerston North, Wellington, Whangarei
    • Hawkes Bay Schools Trades Academy (EIT) – Gisborne and Hastings
    • Land Based Trade Academy (National Trades Academy) – Christchurch
    • Otago Secondary-Tertiary College (Otago Polytechnic) – Cromwell and Dunedin
    • PITA (Taratahi) – North Island
    • Taranaki Trades Academy (WITT)
    • Top of the South Trades Academy (NMIT) – Marlborough and Nelson
    • U-Skills (UCOL) – Masterton, Palmerston North and Wanganui
    • Waikato Trades Academy (Wintec) – Hamilton, Pukekohe and Thames
    • Wellington Trades Academy (Weltec/Whitirea)
    • West Auckland Fono
    • West Coast Trades Academy (Tai Poutini) – Greymouth and Westport.

    In addition, courses are available by distance with PORSE.

    We are continually looking for new partners and opportunities for our students. If you are interested in a programme or provider not listed here, please get in touch.

    What’s in it for me?

    • A hands-on, practical approach to prepare you for work or further tertiary study.
    • An individual learning plan based on your interests and passions.
    • Improved confidence, self-esteem and work readiness.
    • The chance of getting into the workforce and/or trade training faster.

    Who can apply?

    To be eligible to apply for a place on a Huarahi Trades Academy programme, you must:

    • be a secondary school aged student and
    • enrolled with Te Kura in at least three subjects at a minimum of NCEA Level 1.

    This is a full-time commitment over a one to two year period, although some students may achieve their goals over a longer or shorter period. There are no tuition fees.

    Find out more

    If you’re interested in being part of the Huarahi Trades Academy, talk to your Learning Advisor or contact Kathryn Smith on 0800 65 99 88 ext 8552 or email huarahi@tekura.school.nz.
