    About Te Kura

    Our full name is Te Aho o Te Kura Pounamu, but you can call us Te Kura.

    Formerly known as The Correspondence School, Te Kura is New Zealand’s largest school, providing education programmes to more than 23,000 students every year.

    We are based in Wellington, but also have regional offices in Auckland, Hamilton and Christchurch, as well as staff working in communities where there are clusters of Te Kura students, such as Whanganui, Hastings and Nelson. This approach enables us to work alongside communities to support students’ learning.

    Our students come from a wide range of backgrounds with diverse learning needs. They live in locations throughout New Zealand as well as overseas. They may be learning with us full-time, or part-time. Some stay on our roll for just a few months, others for all of their schooling years – and everything in between.

    Distance education for all ages

    Te Kura is a state-funded distance education provider. We offer a wide range of personalised learning programmes and courses, mostly delivered online, from early childhood students to NCEA Level 3. Students can enrol for a few credits in a specific course or for a full-time programme.

    From tiny tots to those aged 90+ – all are welcome, as long as you meet our eligibility criteria.

    Learn at your own pace

    At Te Kura, we tailor your learning programme to fit your needs, so you can learn your way – at your own pace, at your own place. We encourage you to take control of your learning and work out what’s best for you. Don’t like getting out of bed early in the morning? No problem! Start late and finish when it suits you.

    Free for eligible students aged 19 and under

    Enrolment with Te Kura is free if you meet the Ministry of Education’s eligibility criteria. Young adults aged 16 to 19 (and not turning 20 in the year of enrolment) who are not enrolled at another school full-time can enrol with us for free too. Our courses are also available for a small fee to adults aged 20 and over.

    Students who are at a face-to-face school can study courses that aren’t available at their own school. Some eligibility criteria apply.

    Authentic, ‘real-life’ learning opportunities

    Hands-on learning related to your passions and goals is our specialty. Our authentic learning approach gives you the opportunity to explore different career options while gaining new skills and earning NCEA credits.

    ‘One size fits one’ approach

    At Te Kura you receive personalised support, in the comfort of your own home. Our teachers are able to work closely with you one-to-one, and assess how you’re doing as you go, adapting your learning material accordingly.
