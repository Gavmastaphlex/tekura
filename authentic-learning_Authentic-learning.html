    What is authentic learning?

    EMBED AT LEAST ONE AUTHENTIC LEARNING VIDEO AND LINK TO YOUTUBE CHANNEL FOR MORE

    Any suggestions for what video?

    Authentic learning at Te Kura is an approach to learning that focuses on your passions and goals for life beyond school. It uses these as the basis for a learning plan that is unique to you.

    Our authentic learning programmes are designed for students in years 11 to 13 and young adults. You’ll have the chance to work alongside adults in your field of interest and to build knowledge, understanding and skills in that area, as well as work towards a relevant qualification.

    You may also have the opportunity to work with other students online and face to face.

    When a student has a passion we are not limited by a timetable and bells. The students can fully immerse themselves in their passion, create an inquiry/project around it, then dedicate the whole day, week or term learning in that context.

    Jasen, Te Kura teacher

    ‘Real life’ learning

    Examples of authentic learning include:

    • working towards an internship
    • building knowledge and skills in an area that interests you
    • working towards a relevant, practical qualification
    • job shadowing
    • Gateway placements
    • attendance at STAR courses.

    Learn in your community

    Your Te Kura learning advisor will help you to identify your interests and any opportunities in your community where you could take up an internship or Gateway placement.

    Examples of placements include a:

    • local business
    • voluntary or community organisation
    • sports club
    • marae.

    You’ll be supported in your learning by your learning advisor and a workplace mentor, as well as your supervisor and whānau.

    Find out more

    If you’re interested in signing up to an authentic learning programme or just want more information, talk with your learning advisor to see if it’s right for you.
