    Eligibility

    State, state-integrated, partnership schools, kura hourua, and private schools may register Year 1–15 domestic students with Te Kura for government-funded dual tuition where the entry criteria for one of the registration gateways set out in the Ministry of Education’s enrolment policy are met.

    See our eligibility wizard [link when page created] to find out whether your student is eligible or read more below:

    Does your student meet the criteria?

    Small schools

    Secondary schools with fewer than 600 students can register students with Te Kura in up to four subjects under the ‘subject not available’ gateway, as long as the school doesn’t offer the subject and the subject is in the school’s language of tuition (e.g. English or te reo Māori).

    All schools

    Schools of any size are able to register students with Te Kura under the following circumstances:

    • the school has only a small number of students studying the subject at Year 12 or 13 levels
    • students who are gifted and talented and whose home school is unable to provide the curriculum enrichment and acceleration required. Applications must be supported by a current IEP less than six months old (which may be developed with Te Kura in anticipation of an application)
    • a principal of a school unable to offer Māori language tuition may apply to the Ministry of Education to register Year 7–8 students for dual tuition at all levels of te reo Māori. This gateway allows students who have transferred from a Māori medium school or from a school offering level 1 immersion to receive dual tuition in te reo Māori
    • students who are on an approved cultural exchange in New Zealand, require the subjects on return to the country that they are from, and the subject is not available in their New Zealand home school (up to two subjects)
    • students who have a demonstrated family or cultural affinity with a particular ethnic group and require tuition in that language
    • the school has an emergency staff vacancy and no suitable applicants are available (for a minimum of four weeks and a maximum of one term).

    Note: Regional health schools are able to register students with high health needs.

    Special education

    Schools, special schools and government institutions are able to register students for specialist programming or curriculum adaptation.

    For special schools and government institutions, the following criteria apply:

    • dual tuition must be essential to the student’s learning circumstances
    • the special school or institution does not have the capability to meet the learning needs identified, and
    • the capability required is beyond what could reasonably be expected of the school or institution.

    For schools, the student’s programme must be developed in association with Te Kura and contribute to overcoming barriers to achievement as identified in the student’s individual education plan, and to the inclusion of the student in the registering school.

    Schools or institutions receiving this supplementary dual tuition support are expected to undertake a programme of development so they are able to independently provide the specialist programme or curriculum adaptation required.

    Fee-paying students

    Domestic and international students may be registered with Te Kura on a fee-paying basis. The fee is charged to the enrolling school. Refer to our fees schedule for more information. You can complete the enrolment application form online.
