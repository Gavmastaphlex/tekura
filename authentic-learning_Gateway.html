    Gateway

    Gateway is a work experience programme for senior secondary students. With Gateway, you can have a taste of working life while you’re still at school, learn some skills and earn unit standards along the way.

    Who can apply?

    Full-time students in years 11–13 can apply for a place on Gateway. There is a focus on self-directed learning, so we are looking for highly committed students who are motivated, enthusiastic and work ready.

    What qualifications do you get?

    The Gateway programme offers industry unit standards or qualifications that will help you get a job after you leave school.

    How does it work?

    You will work at least 10 days in a workplace that interests you on a regular and ongoing basis. Common industries that our students are placed in are listed below, although it may be possible to work in another field – just let us know what you want to do.

    • agricultural work
    • automotive
    • building and construction
    • dairy farming
    • early childhood
    • electrical
    • equine
    • horticulture
    • hospitality
    • retail

    Over the duration of the course you will receive both on the job training and mentoring from a supervisor. You will also receive an individual development plan and will be required to complete a 20 credit learning plan. This will include theory and sometimes practical work that will be assessed at your workplace. Generally, a Gateway placement is unpaid work.

    How do I apply?

    To express interest, fill out our downloadable Gateway application form [Where is this file?] or apply online. [Create an online application form]Please inform of the fields and where to be submitted.

    Although you can apply at any time, places on Gateway are limited, so you are advised to apply as soon as possible.

    More information about Gateway

    For more information about Gateway, talk to your learning advisor, or email Kathryn Smith or call her on 0800 65 99 88 ext 8552.
