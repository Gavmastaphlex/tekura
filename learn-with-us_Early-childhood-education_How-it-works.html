    How it works

    Te Kura’s early childhood education service is a free individualised programme for children aged three to five (with the option of remaining on until six), designed by registered early childhood teachers based in Wellington.

    Our teachers work in partnership with your family using the New Zealand early childhood curriculum, Te Whāriki to create and develop an individual programme of learning for your child.

    To find out how our distance programme works watch our short video or read on.



    Resources

    You will be sent a range of learning materials by post, which may include books, puzzles, games, audio CDs and DVDs. Some of these will be for your child to keep.

    Ideas and information

    In each posting you’ll receive information about child development, as well as ideas for play activities and learning experiences for your child.

    Online technology

    There are options to use technology and computer resources while enrolled with us. Your teacher will work with you to incorporate these tools into your child’s programme.

    Regular support

    You will build a relationship with your teacher by maintaining regular contact via our free phone number, through email and letters and our online presence, Storypark. If you are visiting Wellington you are welcome to drop in to the Te Kura office and meet your teacher.

    Event days

    Where possible, we hold day events throughout the country that offer you and your children a chance to meet Te Kura teachers and the other families who live in your area.

    Monthly postings

    Generally, early childhood postings contain four weeks’ worth of work. Your child’s teacher will assess returned work and send a new programme of learning tailored to your child’s current interests and learning needs. A return date is generally noted on the comments sheet.
