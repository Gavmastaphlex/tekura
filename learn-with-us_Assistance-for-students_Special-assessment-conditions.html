    Special Assessment Conditions

    Do you need extra support with your learning? Help is at hand! If you have a physical, medical, sensory or learning disability or psychological challenges you may be eligible for assistance with your NCEA assessments. This is known as special assessment conditions (SACSpecial assessment conditions).

    How it works

    SACSpecial assessment conditions include things like being allowed to have someone who is not a family member, family friend or additional teacher/tutor:

    • read your assessment/exam paper to you
    • write the answers dictated by you.

    SACSpecial assessment conditions that are commonly approved for our students include:

    • reader and/or writer (who will come to your home or meet you in a public venue such as a library, community centre or Te Kura advisory)
    • separate room (for exams)
    • computer use
    • Braille papers
    • extra time to complete a timed assessment (such as an exam or test).

    See the NZQA website for the full list of services available.

    How to apply

    You need to be a full-time, adult, fee-paying or overseas student to be eligible to apply for SACSpecial assessment conditions.

    The New Zealand Qualifications Authority (NZQA) has strict guidelines about who is able to have these special arrangements. You will need to provide evidence of your disability from an independent assessment or medical report. Suitable evidence would include:

    • a recent (less than three years) SPELD assessor’s report (less than three years old for learning disabilities and less than one year old for medical, physical and sensory disabilities)
    • a report from a registered psychologist or medical practitioner, depending on the disability.

    Secondary dual student requirements

    If you are a secondary dual student you must apply for SACSpecial assessment conditions through your home school (the school that enrolled you with Te Kura). Please let your Te Kura teachers know if you are approved for SACSpecial assessment conditions.

    Conditions covered

    Conditions covered under SACSpecial assessment conditions include:

    • dyslexia – difficulties with reading and processing words
    • dygraphia – difficulties with written language
    • dyscalculia – difficulties processing numbers
    • dyspraxia – difficulties with motor function
    • anxiety/depression
    • Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)
    • vision impairment
    • cerebral palsy
    • Tourette’s syndrome
    • Asperger’s syndrome.

    See the NZQA website for the full list of conditions covered under SACSpecial assessment conditions.

    Testimonials

    I receive Separate Accommodation assistance. This means that when I go to sit my exams, I am in a classroom by myself or with only a few other students who also require separate accommodation, with an examination supervisor. I have Autism Spectrum Disorder and have spent the last eight years being home schooled through Te Kura. I am not used to being surrounded by a large number of students and am easily distracted by sights, sounds and smells. Separate accommodation takes the stress out of going to a large school to sit examinations. It allows me to manage my anxieties in a quiet calm environment to allow me to achieve to my full potential.

    Matthew, Year 12

    I am able to access the use of a laptop for my exams, which makes a huge difference. Educational testing has shown that my brain wiring is more suited to working on a computer, so being able to use a laptop means my results actually reflect what I know. It’s such a relief to know I have the best chance of succeeding in my exams.

    Monica, Year 12
