    STAR

    If you are interested in a specific career, a Secondary Tertiary Alignment Resource (STAR) course may help you work out if it’s right for you. STAR courses are short courses held at polytechnics or private training providers for secondary school students.

    How does it work?

    You will participate in a short-term programme or 'taster-course' for a few days at a time, or one day a week for several weeks.

    Popular courses include:

    • barista training
    • cooking
    • carpentry
    • first aid training.

    Who can apply for a STAR course?

    Full-time students and young adults enrolled in three or more subjects can apply. Preference is given to year 11–13 students but sometimes year 10 students are allowed. If accepted for a STAR course, your fees will be covered by Te Kura.

    Travel subsidy

    STAR students may apply for a travel subsidy. If your travel is by public transport you will need to provide copies of your tickets or a receipt of purchase together with a travel subsidy form. If you travel by car, you will need to record the mileage. For more information contact Chris Berentson.

    Each year Te Kura offers two annual residential camps for full-time Te Kura students. Our North Island camp is held at Hillary Outdoors, near Mt Ruapehu, and the South Island camp is held at Boyle River in the Lewis Pass.

    If you are in year 11 or above, you are welcome to apply to attend this course. There are 20 funded places available, paid for by Te Kura.

    Activities on camp include ropes courses, climbing wall, flying fox, caving, navigation, mountain walking, canoeing, rock climbing and abseiling.
