    Key dates

    You should also familiarise yourself with key dates in 2017 related to NCEA. As well as telling you when to expect your results, this includes dates or deadlines for:

    • NCEA payment and registration
    • exams (including practice exams)
    • internal assessments
    • portfolio submissions.

    If you are enrolled with another school, make sure you are familiar with our NCEA assessment information sheet, which contains deadlines and information specific to students doing NCEA through Te Kura.

    Exam timetable

    Visit the NZQA website for the 2017 exam timetable.

    Note: Practice exam timetables will be emailed to you when they are ready, or you can find them on our Calendar and Events page.

    Check your NCEA results

    • Log in to NZQA to check your latest results. To register for this service you’ll need to enter your National Student Number and date of birth to confirm your identity. Contact your learning advisor if you don’t know your National Student Number.
    • NCEA Student is a mobile app that will help you plan your NCEA study programme, set goals and track your progress.

    What if I don’t agree with an assessment grade?

    To appeal an assessment decision, fill in our Student Assessment Appeal Form and email or post it to your teacher with supporting documentation.
